Important things to know!

  • 01. Patience

    Anthotypes need sometimes months for an exposure and not all substances will produce a print. It is a realm of experimentation.

  • 02. Precautions

    Researching the flower, plant or even mushroom you wish to use, to ensure it's safe! Many plants are poisonous or even deadly.

  • 03. Results

    The image can be somewhat faint. Images are monochrome, one color. They fade over time.

  • 04. Benefits

    • Environmentally Friendly
    • Fun way to experiment
    • Wonderful smells (most of the time!)
    • Unique

Swiss Chard Anthotype

Skill level: Easy

Expsoure time 4-8 hours in bright sunlight

How to make a Swiss Chard Anthotype

You need a 4–5 leaves to make 8″ x 10″ print

How to make a Swiss Chard Anthotype

Discard the stems

You can cut the stems up and cook them, there completely good and healthy.

Add salt and leave for 10 mins.
This will assist in extracting the spinach emulsion.

After blending.

Use some old material or clothes to squeeze out the liquid

Swiss Chard Anthotype liquid in glass tray.

Let paper soak in the juices of vegetables and paint on the juice emlusion with a hake brush.

Swiss Chard Anthotype - Hake brush

Let it dry over night and use the next day.

Swiss Chard

Produces good prints however fades rather quickly

  • Exposure: 4-8 hours bright sunlight
  • Longevity:Fades will in days

  • How-To

    I found using a piece of plate glass and 2cm thick wooden board bought me the best results. I had tried in the contact frame but there was too much condensation and less ways to release it. With a simpler approach the glass plate and wooden board clipped together allowed moisture build up to evaporate through the open sides of the makeshift sandwich.
